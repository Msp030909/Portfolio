FRACTURED

FRACTURED is probably one of the projects I am most proud of. There's reason why I talk about it both here and in the home page. It was my first real website, and a lot of work was put into making it like how it is now. My goal while renovating was not only making it look nicer and making it more interactive using Javascript, but also making the text paint a better picture of what FRACTURED is supposed to be.

For context, FRACTURED is based on a game idea I had some time ago. Inspired by genres like roguelikes, the game would put you on a hubworld and let you freely explore. In a butterfly-effect fashion, any actions you made while in the hub would lead you into a more linear path with it's own unique storyline, eventually leading you back to the hub to try again.. Everything, from places you explore, enemies you fight, and weapons you use, would be different depending on what choices you make. This means that, for example, playing as a thief would give you an arsenal more oriented towards stealth, while playing as a soldier would give you lots of firepower.

Above all, I wanted for the website to convey the game well, and this is something I think I accomplished. There's lots of mockup images of the game, and some descriptive text to go along with it. However, the point of this website was also to test my skills with HTML, CSS, and Javascript, which is why a lot of the features, like randomized fun facts and some secrets, were added.
